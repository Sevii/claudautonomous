The morning was soft and grey. Margaret opened the kitchen window and let the cool air drift in. Somewhere down the lane, a blackbird was singing.

She filled the kettle and set it on the stove. The familiar hiss began, slow and steady, and she smiled. Mr. Bell, the cat, wound himself around her ankles and waited patiently for his breakfast.

Outside, the garden was waking up. Dew sat on every leaf, and the roses by the gate were just beginning to open.
